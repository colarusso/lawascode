2015 ABA/NLADA Equal Justice Works Conference

Pro Bono: How to Power Your Pro Bono Practice With Apps

May 8, 2015, Austin, Texas (1:45pm - 3:15pm)

In this session (part 2), a panel of software developers and attorneys will explore how public service attorneys can leverage technology to improve their practice, including: (1) how to frame a viable app idea for developers; (2) how to teach yourself to code; (3) what's involved in building an app; and (4) what resources are available to assist attorneys in making their pro bono "dream apps" (appellate or otherwise) a reality.

David Colarusso

David Colarusso is a public defender, software engineer, and former high school physics teacher living in the greater Boston area. He was hired by the MA public defender agency (CPCS) as a staff attorney but spends most of his time doing data science and business analysis. He is the author of a programing language for lawyers, QnA Markup, and along with teammates William Li and David Zvenyach, he created an app for the determination of indecency and calculation of sentencing (Due Processr) as part of the ABA’s inaugural hackathon. He likes to build things: furniture, software, reasoned arguments... You can find his latest creations on his website.

David Zvenyach
Web: esq.io

V. David Zvenyach is a Senior Advisor at 18F within the US General Services Administration. Before joining the federal government, David served as the General Counsel to the Council of the District of Columbia.

David is an active member of the D.C. Bar, Washington Council of Lawyers, and the Uniform Law Commission. He is also an “accomplished armchair coder,” building web applications that make it easier to access, understand, track, and edit legal documents. For these efforts, David was recently named an Open Data Pioneer by the Open Data Institute, the Legal Hacker of the Year by the DC Legal Hackers, and was a part of the 2014 class of the FastCase 50, where has was described as “a model for other open law advocates inside government.”

He previously served as Chief of Staff to D.C. Councilmember Mary Cheh, and as a law clerk for Chief Judge Yvette Kane in the United States District Court in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania. Dave is a graduate of the George Washington Law School and holds a mechanical engineering degree from the University of Wisconsin.

David lives in DC with his spouse and two children.
