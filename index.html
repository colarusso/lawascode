Law as Code

QnA Markup and Legal Hackathons et al.

QnA Markup is a simple computer language written for lawyers with little or no programming experience. It transforms blocks of text into interactive question and answer sessions (QnAs). These QnAs can be used as stand-alone expert systems or in the aid of rule-based document construction. Plus, they can be fun, and the entire project is open source. Among other things, that means free. QnA is ideal for lawyers and law students looking to turn themselves into attorney-coders.

The following is a crowd-sourced list of classes and legal hackathons making use of QnA. To add to the list or learn more about how to host your own hackathon, email hackathon@qnamarkup.org.
